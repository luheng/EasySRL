We want to teach our smart robot to understand human language and we need your help! The robot will ask you questions when it’s confused about the structure of a sentence. For example, given the sentence:

Stockholders who took the hint and sold shares escaped the October debacle.

The robot might ask:

What escaped something?

along with the following options as possible answers:

  • stockholders
  • the hint
  • shares
  • None of the above.

You need to choose every correct answer. In this case, there is just one: stockholders.

Guidelines

  1. Part of the question is highlighted in the sentence to make the task easier. But please read the whole sentence before making you choice.
  2. Choose the options that explicitly, directly and accurately answer the question (See examples #1-#3).
  3. Check multiple options if all of them are needed to answer the question, otherwise pick the shortest one that satisfies Guideline No. 2  (See examples #2, #4, #5).
  4. Select “None of the above” if 1) none of the listed answers is good enough, 2) the question is nonsensical, or 3) the question is not answerable given the information in the sentence. (See example #6)
  5. Please read the examples carefully, as they will help you understand the task better.


Important Notes: There are two kinds of errors in questions that you should forgive.

  1. The robot may make mistakes between "who" and "what," and "someone" and "something." A question such as "What likes something" can still be answered with  "I", if that's what the sentence says.
  2. The robot might forget to say "not" in the question. For example, given the sentence "I refuse to eat cake" and the question "What eats something?", the answer should be "I".

In these cases, do not necessarily mark "None of the above". Instead, proceed as though the error had not been made.

Example #1:

I like many kinds of fruit, including apples and oranges.
- What does someone like?
  • many kinds of fruit
  • apples
  • oranges
  • None of the above.

You should only answer many kinds of fruit  for this question. Even though you could conclude that "I like apples" is true, and likewise for the other answers, they are not stated directly in the sentence. 


Example #2:

Japan's domestic sales of cats in October, as they reported, rose 300% from a year earlier.
- What rose?
  • Japan 's domestic
  • Japan 's domestic sales of cats in October
  • sales
  • cats
  • None of the above.

The best answer should be sales, because *sales* is the thing that rose. cats and Japan's domestic  are inaccurate because they are not what the question is asking about. Japan's domestic sales of cats in October also answers the question, but we favor the shorter answer in this case.


Example #3:

Knowing a five-star meal when they eat one, the executives gave the chef a standing ovation.
- What would something eat?
  • a five-star meal
  • one
  • the executives
  • None of the above.

The only correct answer is one. The answer a five-star meal is incorrect, because it is not as direct (even though it refers to the same thing as the correct answer). 


Example #4:

They provided free checking, a safe-deposit box and travelers checks for only $3 a month.
- What did something provide?
  • free checking
  • safe-deposit box
  • travelers checks
  • $3
  • None of the above.

Correct answers are free checking, safe-deposit box, and travelers checks, because all these three things are stated to be provided. $3 is  incorrect because it isn't among the things being provided.


Example #5:

Mr. Johnson, the vice chairman, was elected director of the Research and Development Dept.
- What was elected something?
  • Mr. Johnson
  • the vice chairman
  • director
  • None of the above.

This is a special case where the sentence lists an entity (a person, company, etc.) with a phrase next to it referring to the same thing in a different way. In this case, please check *both* answers: Mr. Johnson and the vice chairman.


Example #6:

They provided free checking, a safe-deposit box and travelers checks for only $3 a month.
- What checks something?
  • they
  • travelers
  • None of the above.

The correct answer is None of the above, since the question is nonsensical in the context of the question.
