We want to teach our smart robot to understand human language and we need your help! The robot will ask you questions when it’s confused about the structure of a sentence. For example, given the sentence:

Stockholders who took the hint and sold shares escaped the October debacle.

The robot might ask:

What escaped something?

along with the following options as possible answers:

  • stockholders
  • the hint
  • shares
  • None of the above.

You need to choose every correct answer. In this case, there is just one: stockholders.

Guidelines

  1. Choose the options that explicitly, directly and accurately answer the question (See examples #1-#3).

  2. Check multiple options if all of them are correct answers to the question (See examples #4 and #5).

  3. Select “None of the above” if 1) None of the listed answers is good enough. or 2) Question is nonsensical. or 3) Question is not answerable given information in the sentence.

Important Notes: There are two kinds of errors in a question that you should forgive.

  1. The robot may make mistakes between "who" and "what," and "someone" and "something." Therefore, a question such as "What likes something" can still be answered with  "I", if that's what the sentence says.
  2. The robot might forget to say "not" in the question. For example, given sentence "I refuse to eat cakes." and question "What eats something?", the answer should still be "I".

In these cases, do not necessarily mark "None of the above" in these cases. Instead, proceed as though this error had not been made.

Examples

Criterion 1:  Choose the options that explicitly, directly  and accurately answer the question.

Example #1:

I like many kinds of fruit, including apples and oranges.
- What do someone like?
  • many kinds of fruit
  • apples
  • oranges
  • None of the above.

You should only answer many kinds of fruit  for this question. Even though you could conclude that "I like apples" is true, and likewise for the other answers, they are not stated directly in the sentence. 

 More generally, if one option more directly answers the question than another option, only choose the more direct and accurate one, as in the following example.


Example #2:

Japan 's domestic sales of cars in October, as they reported, rose 18% from a year earlier.

- What rose?
  • Japan 's domestic
  • Japan 's domestic sales of cars in October, as they reported
  • sales
  • cars
  • None of the above.

Among the listed, the only correct answer should be sales, because "sales rose 18%". Other answers are either indirect (cars, because the question is not directly asking about cars) or inaccurate (Japan 's domestic, which is different from "sales" or Japan 's domestic sales of cars in October, as they reported, which contains irrelevant information).


Example #3:

Knowing a five-star meal when they eat one, the executives gave the chef a standing ovation.
- What would something eat?
  • a five-star meal
  • one
  • the executives
  • None of the above.

the only correct answer is one. The answer a five-star meal is incorrect, because it is not as direct (even though the phrase refers to the same thing as the correct answer). For question

- Who would eat something?

the most direct answer is they, rather than the executives.

Criterion 2:  Check multiple options if all of them are correct answers to the question.

Example #4:

They provided free checking, a safe-deposit box and travelers checks for only $3 a month.
- What did something provide?
  • free checking
  • safe-deposit box
  • travelers checks
  • $3
  • None of the above.

Correct answers are free checking, safe-deposit box, and travelers check, because all these three things are *provided*.  On the other hand, $3 is an incorrect answer, because it's not among the things that are provided.


Example #5:

Mr. Johnson, vice chairman, was elected director of the Research and Development Dept.
- What was elected something?
  • mr. Johnson
  • vice chairman
  • director
  • None of the above.

This is a special case where the sentence lists an entity (a person, company, etc.) with phrases next to it referring to the same thing. In this case, please check *both* answers: mr. Johnson and vice chairman.

Criterion 3:  Check multiple options if all of them are correct answers to the question.

Example #6:

They provided free checking, a safe-deposit box and travelers checks for only $3 a month.
- What checks something?
  • they
  • travelers
  • None of the above.

Correct answer is None of the above, since the question is nonsensical under the context of the question.
