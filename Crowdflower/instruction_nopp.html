Reading Questions

The robot-generated questions are not perfect. There are sometimes mistakes in tense, capitalization and choice of words (e.g., "who" vs. "what", "someone" vs. "something", or forgetting a "not"). In case of these minor errors, please try your best to give the correct answer. However, if you think the question is nonsensical, or is not answerable given the sentence, please choose the option Question is not valid. 

For example, given the sentence:

They included free checking, a safe-deposit box and travelers checks for only $3 a month.

Here is a valid question:

What did they include?

However, the following question:

What would safe-deposit travelers checks?

is not valid. Because it is neither grammatical, nor answerable given the sentence.

Choosing the Best Answer

There may be multiple answer options that are related to the question, but you need to choose the one that most accurately answers the question. For example, given the sentence:

Japan 's domestic sales of cars in October rose 18% from a year earlier to 500,004 units.

and the question:

What rose?

Correct answers would include: "Japan 's domestic sales of cars", "domestic sales", "sales". Wrong answers would include: "Japan",  "Japan 's domestic", "cars". Because according to the sentence, "sales" rose, not "cars" or "Japan". Usually there is only one correct answer among listed options. When there are multiple correct answers, please choose the shortest correct one, such as:

  1. Japan's domestic sales of cars
  2. domestic sales
  3. cars
  4. Answer not listed
  5. Question is not valid

Given the sentence:

Knowing a tasty meal when they eat one , the executives gave the chefs a standing ovation .

and question:

What would they eat?

the best, and most direct answer is "one", instead of "a tasty meal". Because although the latter contains more information,  "one" is the directly and explicitly mentioned by the sentence as in the part "... when they eat one".

Similarly, the most direct answer for the question

What would eat one?

is "they", instead of "the executives".

Multiple Applicable Answers

Sometimes a question has multiple answers. These will be separated by a bold slash symbol / within the answer choices. For example, given the sentence:

They included free checking, a safe-deposit box and travelers checks for only $3 a month.

and the question:

What did they include?

the following answer:

free checking  /  a safe-deposit box  /  travelers checks

is a better answer than the other candidates such as:

free checking, a safe-deposit box and travelers checks
free checking  /  a safe-deposit box

As a special case, when the sentence lists a person/company with a describing phrase next to it, such as:

J.P. Bolduc, vice chairman of W.R. Grace & Co., was elected a director.

Please choose an answer that lists both of them, such as:

J.P. Bolduc  /  vice chairman of W.R. Grace & Co.

If you think there is a correct answer to the question, but none of the listed ones is acceptable, please go with the option Answer is not listed.
